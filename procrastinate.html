--- title: Why do we procrastinate? description: It seems so counterintuitive, and yet, so many of us do it. Why is this, and how can we stop it? --- {% seo %}

I have a confession to make.

I procrastinated making this. And in terms of putting something off, I'm not alone. Around twenty percent of americans are chronic procrastinators, and 70 to 90 percent of undergraduates claim to be.

It's a problem that has nagged humans for centuries.

Speaking about procrastination, the greek poet Hesiod said “put your work off till tomorrow and the day after.” in 800 b.c. That's more than 2000 years before polio. And we've eradicated polio. But yet, procrastination still plagues us.
